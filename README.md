## What is Injection Mold Design?

Injection mold design is the engineering process of creating a precision tool
(mold) used to produce plastic parts through injection molding. A well-designed
mold determines part quality, cycle time, production cost, and tool longevity.

Key design considerations include:
- Part geometry and wall thickness
- Draft angles and undercuts
- Gate location and runner layout
- Cooling channel design
- Ejection system

---

## Types of Injection Molds

| Mold Type | Description | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Single-cavity** | One part per cycle | Prototypes, low volume |
| **Multi-cavity** | Multiple identical parts per cycle | High-volume production |
| **Family mold** | Different parts in one mold | Assembly sets |
| **Insert mold** | Metal inserts molded into plastic | Threaded parts, connectors |
| **Overmold** | Second material over base part | Soft-grip handles, seals |
| **Hot runner** | No runner waste, faster cycles | High-volume, precision |
| **Cold runner** | Simple, lower tooling cost | General production |

## DFM Analysis

Design for Manufacturability (DFM) analysis is conducted before mold
fabrication to identify potential issues:

- **Wall thickness** — Uniform walls prevent warping and sink marks
- **Draft angles** — Minimum 1–3° for easy part ejection
- **Undercuts** — Require side actions or lifters; increase mold cost
- **Ribs and bosses** — Must follow thickness ratio rules
- **Parting line** — Placement affects appearance and flash risk

## Gate & Runner Systems

### Gate Types
- **Edge gate** — Most common, simple, leaves small mark
- **Submarine gate** — Auto-degating, no post-processing
- **Pin point gate** — Hot runner systems, minimal gate mark
- **Fan gate** — Wide parts, reduces stress
- **Valve gate** — Precise control, premium surface finish

### Runner Systems
- **Cold runner** — Lower cost, material waste
- **Hot runner** — Higher tooling cost, zero waste, faster cycles

|----------------|----------------------|--------------|

| Precision | ±0.05mm | Electronics, connectors |
| High precision | ±0.02mm | Medical devices, optics |

## Mold Lifecycle & Maintenance

- **Standard steel molds**: 500,000–1,000,000 shots
- **Hardened steel molds**: 1,000,000+ shots
- **Preventive maintenance**: Every 50,000–100,000 shots
- **Key maintenance tasks**: Cleaning, lubrication, inspection of wear surfaces,
  cooling channel flushing
